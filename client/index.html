Canada's housing crisis is far more complicated than most people think.

Today, housing prices have skyrocketed and young people moving out are paying up to 30% more on housing than people 50 years ago.

However, a large majority of the older population's retirement and wealth is tied into their properties, and depend on their property value increasing to live. This dichotomy fuels a political scene that naturally moves to inaction, as doing too much for one side hurts the other.

We strongly believe first step to making meaningful change is having a solid understanding of the issue at hand, and highly encourage everyone to spend more time educating themselves on the topic.
